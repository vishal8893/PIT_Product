IMPORTANT GUIDANCE ON NUVAMA CODES ON PROHIBITION OF INSIDER TRADING

Please take a few minutes to read these important points with regards to the Prohibition of Insider Trading Codes and the Dos and Don'ts of Employee Trading and confirm your acceptance.

As you are aware, Nuvama has revised the Code for Prohibition of Insider trading in Nuvama Wealth Management Limited (NWML) and other listed securities (Non NWML) for dealing in securities; in line with amendments to SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015 effective April 01, 2019

Every employee of Nuvama group and their Immediate Relatives/Connected Entities or Persons /Dependents have to adhere to the Codes in place at all times while dealing in securities
In Nuvama, we have following Codes and FAQs governing employee trading:
  1. Code For Prohibition Of Insider Trading In The Securities Of Nuvama Wealth Management Limited (NWML Code) Click here to know more
  2. Nuvama Code for trading in other listed securities (Non NWML Code) Click here to know more
  3. FAQs on Both Codes Click here to know more
You may refer to the Codes/FAQs in detail at Nuvama Ping >Policies > Compliance

Changes to note (effective April 01, 2019)
  1. There may be changes to your categorisation i.e. Designated or Non-Designated category under both the Codes
  2. You (including your Immediate Relatives and Connected Persons) will continue to be categorised separately as Designated or Non-Designated under both the Codes. However, as per revised Codes, you will be a "Designated Employee" if you are CEO or Business Head of an Nuvama Group Company or 2 levels below that. In addition, the Company may also treat you as a "Designated Employee" due to various criteria such as your designation, function, role, etc. Your exact status will be available on EIRF system effective April 01, 2019.
  3. You will be given notice to refrain from trading in securities of any Listed Company (including NWML), in case you are part of any transaction, deal or handle certain activities; by virtue of which you may have access to or possess Unpublished Price Sensitive Information (UPSI) about the Company.
  4. In case you receive notice as above, you shall ensure that you do not execute contra trade in the securities of such Listed Company for a period of six months. E.g. If you bought scrip A on Jan 01, 2019, and you get involved in a deal or transaction pertaining to this scrip on Jan 02, 2019, you will ensure not to execute contra trade i.e sell scrip A on or before July 02, 2019, or till the time the UPSI is made public, whichever is later.
  5. In case you are a Non-Designated Employee, you will need to seek pre-clearance in case you wish to trade in any scrip above the value of Rs 10 lacs (per day-per scrip).

Important Points to note
  1. The Codes are applicable to you and your Immediate Relatives/Dependents and Connected Person/Entity
  2. Spouse is "Deemed Dependent" under both Codes irrespective of financial status or whether he/she consults you for trading decisions. Pre-clearance and all other requirements of both Codes as applicable to you shall also apply to your spouse for trades undertaken by him / her
  3. Pre-clearance for all securities (including NWML) is valid only for one day
  4. Pre-clearance is required for listed Debt securities/REITS/INVTS in addition to all other securities as mentioned in the Codes
  5. Pre-clearance and Holding Period requirements are applicable to Discretionary Portfolio Management Services (DPMS)
  6. You are required to trade only through Nuvama Wealth Management Limited / Nuvama Wealth and Investment Limited, the only exception being in case of a Financially Independent Spouse who is allowed to trade through a broker of their choice.
  7. Trading Window Closure for NWML securities shall continue to apply to all employees and their Relatives/Dependents and Connected Person/Entity irrespective of whether you are Designated or Non-Designated under the NWML Code
  8. You are required to submit declarations/undertakings, including Annual declaration for self as well as your Immediate Relatives/Dependents and Connected Person/Entity, as required under the relevant Code

Do's Dont's
  • Before procuring or sharing any information pertaining to any listed security, ensure that it is not an Unpublished Price Sensitive Information (UPSI)
  • Before trading, ensure that you are not in possession of any UPSI with regards to the security in which you intend to trade.
  • Check whether you are a Designated Employee under both or any of the Codes. If yes, follow all the trading restrictions and other disclosure requirements laid down in the respective Code/s
  • Ensure you inform your Immediate Relatives and Connected Persons about Codes and also seek preclearance on their behalf as required
  • Ensure you take preclearance of trades undertaken by your spouse, irrespective of financial status or whether he/she consults you for trading decisions
  • Trading in NWML securities by any employee as well as their Immediate Relative/Dependent/ Connected Person/Entity is strictly prohibited during the period of Trading Window Closure.
  • Do not communicate any UPSI pertaining to NWML or any other listed company to any other person in any form including social media, chat etc.
  • Do not trade in securities when you are in possession of UPSI
  • In case you are a Designated Person as per NWML Code or hold UPSI with regards to other listed company, do not undertake any trade contrary to the last trade within a period of 6 months.
  • Do not undertake intraday trades (except for trading in Index/Currency/Commodity derivatives)
